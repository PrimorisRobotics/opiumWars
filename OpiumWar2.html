The Second Opium War

Short Summary

Britain has been trading with China for a long time now.

Full Overview

The Second Opium War didn't officially start until October of 1856, but Febuary of the same year a French missionary was killed in Guangxi, China, so it all was very tense. The thing that officially started the Second Opium War was Chinese officials boarding a British-registered ship and allegedly lowering the British flag. At that time, Britan was looking for an excuse to start another war to get even more trading rights with the Chinese. in response to that ship incedent, Britan sent a battleship to Canton, and the war begins. France decides to use the missionary killed earlier as an excuse to join Britan in the war, hoping to get some French trading rights in China as well. Later in December of 1856, the Chinese decide to attempt to make a power move and burn some foriegn factories in Canton. nothing happens for about a year until December of the following year, where France and Britan decide to combine forces and overwhelm Canton, quickly capturing it. In May of 1858, Russia and China make a deal so that only those two nations can cross the Amur, Ussuri, and Sungari rivers. on May 20, 1858 the British and French capture the Dagu forts on the Hai River. from June to November of 1858 the hostilities between the three countries end, where the French and British get what they wanted and a little more, where Opium (remember that from the first opium war, folks?) is leagalized in China.
